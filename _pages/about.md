I am a Principal AI Engineer at [K-Dense](https://k-dense.ai), where I build AI systems that accelerate biological research at scale. I completed my PhD at [MIT](https://mit.edu) in 2025 in [Brain and Cognitive Sciences](https://bcs.mit.edu/), working with [Prof. Josh McDermott](https://web.mit.edu/jhm/www/) in the [Laboratory for Computational Audition](https://mcdermottlab.mit.edu/). K-Dense is backed by the Google AI Futures Fund and Accel Atoms program.

My research spans two domains connected by a shared instinct: building rigorous generative models of complex physical and biological systems.

Current Work (K-Dense)
======

At K-Dense I lead AI research at the intersection of transcriptomics, aging biology, and autonomous scientific analysis.

**Transcriptomic Aging Clocks**
I built an ensemble aging clock trained on 57,584 samples across 28 tissues (ages 1-114 years), achieving R2 = 0.854 and MAE = 4.26 years with calibrated uncertainty intervals. Stage-specific aging markers discovered include CDKN2A/p16, AMPD3, MIR29B2CHG, and SEPTIN3.

**GeneJepa**
A predictive world model of the transcriptome. [bioRxiv, 2025]

**Revive-Flow**
A foundation model for blood DNA methylation aging. [bioRxiv, 2025]

**K-Dense Analyst**
An autonomous AI system for scientific analysis that compresses months of research into weeks. [arXiv, 2025]

PhD Research (MIT, 2019-2025)
======

My doctoral work studied **auditory intuitive physics**: how humans perceive physical properties of objects (material, mass, size, surface texture) from the sounds they make. I focused on rigid-body contact sounds including impacts, scrapes, and rolls.

Key contributions include differentiable physical synthesis models for scraping and rolling sounds, sample-efficient learning of auditory object representations, and behavioral experiments probing how listeners solve the inverse problem from sound to physical cause.

**Thesis (2025):** *Synthesis and perception of sounds from physical interactions reveals auditory intuitive physics*

In summer 2023, I interned at **Meta Reality Labs Research** on spatial audio rendering for smart glasses.

Before MIT, I earned a BTech and MTech in Mechanical Engineering from **IIT Bombay**, with a focus on acoustics and automation. I worked with Prof. Sripriya Ramamoorthy on inverse acoustic characterization of periodic foams, and with researchers at CNRS Marseille and Simon Fraser University on sound synthesis and music transcription.
